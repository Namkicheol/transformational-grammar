1) It seems that John is honest.
>> The situation that the verb seem describes is not about the individual John,
but rather about the proposition that John is honest.

2) Since control verbs like try and hope require their subject to have an agent
role, an expletive it or there, which takes no semantic role, cannot function as
their subject.

3) Since the raising adjective easy do not assign any semantic role to its
subject, we can have it as its subject. On the other hand, the control adjective
eager assigns a role and thus does not allow the expletive it as its subject.

4) A control predicate requires its subject to be able to bear the semantic role
of  agent.

5) The verb thank requires a human subject and an object that is at least
animate.

6) Unlike the verb is, understands require its subject to be sentient.

7) Subject of  a raising predicate is that of  the infinitival VP complement, and it
has no semantic role at all coming from the raising predicate.

8) The control predicate eager assigns a semantic role to its subject, and this
forces A and B to differ semantically: in A, it is the dentist who is eager to
examine Pat, whereas in B, it is Pat who is eager to be examined by the dentist.

9) Unlike believe, persuade does not license an expletive object ( just like try
does not license an expletive subject ).

10) While the idiomatic reading is retained with the raising verb believed, it is
lost with the control verb persuaded.

Coordination with noun heads
When heads are coordinated, the usual interpretation is that the determiner, premodiﬁer, and
postmodiﬁer apply to each of the conjoins:

his wife and child (= his wife and his child)
old men and women (= old men and old women)
some cows and pigs from our farm
(= some cows from our farm and some pigs from our farm)
the boys and girls staying at the hostel
(= the boys staying at the hostel and the girls staying at the hostel)

It is also possible to interpret some of these phrases as coordinated noun phrases:
old men and women (= women and old men)
some cows and pigs from our farm (= pigs from our farm and some cows)

a dishonest and lazy student (= a student who is both dishonest and lazy)

VP에 적절히 남길 만한 조동사가 없다면 do의 적절한 형태가 들어가줘야 한다.

Ex) John my have gone home for the summer, and Alicia may have gone home for the
summer, too.
John may have gone home for the summer, and Alicia may have, too.
( 왜 여기는 have까지 남기냐? 안그러면 뜻이 달라질 수도 있어~)
* John may have gone home for the summer, and Alicia may, too.
>> John may have gone home for the summer, and Alicia may go home for the summer, too.

VP gapping : more than just one verb can be elided. ( VP ellipsis는 조동사가 남아야 하니까 이걸로 구분 )
Ex) She came to Cananda in 1999, and her parents came to Canada in 2001.
She came to Canada in 1999, and her parents in 2001. ( 조동사가 X )
Gapping cannot be applied to sentences with infinitive or gerund complements.
John asked if Bill would leave, and Sam asked if Sue would apologize. John asked if Bill would leave, and Sam if Sue would apologize. 가능
John asked Bill to leave, and Sam asked Sue to apologize. * John asked Bill to leave, and Sam Sue to apologize.

Shared constituent coordination
똑같은 성분 마지막으로 다 보내기
Ex) Alex owned a vintage 1939 Bentley, and Sue knew a guy who wanted to buy a vintage 1939 Bentley.
Alex owned, and Sue knew a guy who wanted to buy,  a vintage 1939 Bentley.

Subject - verb agreement
The main verb should agree in number with the noun phrase that immediately precedes it.
Ex) Neither the president nor the members of the cabinet were informed.
* Neither the president  nor the members of the cabinet was informed.

Joint coordination
Combinatory : 이미 하나이기에 따로 분리 불가능 ex) Alex and Chris are happy couple. =/= * Alex is a happy couple, and Chris is a happy couple.
Joint coordination은 항상 and만 사용 가능 ex) * Alex and probably Chris as well are a happy couple.

Sentences with two NPs joined by ‘and’ in subject position is ambiguous.
Steven and Geo have made a lot of successful movies.
1. Steve has made a lot of successful movies, and Geo has made a lot of successful movies. Distributive / segregatory coordination
2. Steve and Geo together have made a lot of successful movies. Combinatory
